Objective

Our paramount aim with "Hypoxia" is to inject a fresh perspective into the F-O-R-M (Festival of Recorded Movement), captivating a diverse audience. Our focus is on dismantling stereotypical views and disciplines of women, establishing a deep emotional connection with our viewers. This project is meticulously crafted to resonate with artists, activists, and females of all ages care about feminism.

Theme

"Hypoxia" draws inspiration from personal experiences and acute observations. Our thematic focus centers around portraying the suffocating and self-surviving experience of young women navigating societal standards, delving into the conflicts between external expectations and internal thoughts.

The intent is to translate these emotions into visually compelling body expressions for heightened emotional engagement.

Inspiration

“F-O-R-M 2020 Commission: Sunglow Gecko”: This inspired our approach to movement, costume, and space design. We sought to infuse a sense of liberation into our narrative, challenging the confinements imposed by societal norms.

““F-O-R-M 2020 Commission: LUNACY”: The exploration of female movement and symbolism in societal situations influenced our visual storytelling. We aimed to depict the struggles and conflicts faced by women within our narrative.

“MY BODY TO ME｜身体十问”: This minimalist contemporary dance piece informed our understanding of disciplines and standards on the female body. It inspired us to delve into the emotional aspect of the female experience, translating it into our visual narrative.

Challenge

I. Limited Filming Location Access

Unexpected building closures during holidays prompted a schedule overhaul, requiring quick decision-making and communication with the team.

II. Inconsistent Lighting

Filming in Burnaby, BC, with unpredictable natural light and inadequate lighting equipment posed a challenge, especially given our tight two-day shooting schedule.

My Solution

I. Adaptable Schedule

I promptly restructured the shooting schedule after communicated with the building manager, and efficiently communicated changes to the production and acting team, ensuring minimal disruption.

II. Lighting Adaptations

After Day 1's shooting, I strategically re-arranged the shot order for Day 2 to better align with available lighting conditions, which demonstrate my adaptability and problem-solving skills.
